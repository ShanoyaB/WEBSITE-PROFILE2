Reflect

From the book “Twelve Pillars”, it is a narrative fictional work that emphasizes the personal development principles one should have in life. Furthermore, each chapter explores different aspects of personal development through fictional characters, presenting relatable situations that resonate with readers. The book’s main characters its centered around is Micheal; 40-year-old man and Charlies; elderly 70-year-old man. Charlies provides detailed guidance on how to achieve a meaningful life, such as choosing friendships and associations wisely, or even personal growth begins with you and your discipline to change. Hence, these tools and words of wisdom can be applied in our lives to further our personal growth as individuals



Summary

o Chapter 4:
- This chapter is titled “Achieve Your Goals”. Firstly, Michael arranged a meeting with Charlie at a diner where they talked about how to achieve goals. Charlie recommends that Michael write down his goals; a hundred goals to be exact. This led to further discussion on making goals and the steps needed to turn dreams into reality. Furthermore, this is a cornerstone of success where the chapter provides timeless strategies for defining your vision, staying focused, and taking action to achieve the success you desire. And for this to happen, investment of time and effort into oneself, which includes developing new skills, expanding knowledge, and cultivating positive habits.

o Chapter 5:
- This chapter is titled “The Proper Use of Time”. Still under the setting of the diner, Charlie’s provides another pillar of success to Micheal where they focus on the importance of proper time management. Moreover, the chapter emphasizes that time is a precious resource that should be utilized wisely to achieve personal growth and success. “……something will master and something will serve. Either you run the day or the day runs you. Either you control your time or it controls you. Take your pick…” quoted from the book that Charlie spoke towards Micheal about investing time in oneself, to develop new skills, cultivate positive habits to enhance their value in the marketplace and open doors to new possibilities.

o Chapter 6:
- This chapter is titled “Surround Yourself With the Best People”. Uniquely, this chapter takes place at the house of Mr. Davis, where Charlie provides again another set of wisdom to Michael on the importance of surrounding oneself with the best people. Hence the emphasis that the people you choose to be with will influence and impact your success as well as happiness. The chapter encourages readers to seek out individuals who share their values and goals, as their presence can provide support, inspiration, and motivation; “……. To attract attractive people you must be attractive. To attract powerful people you must be powerful. To attract committed people you must be committed……” quoted from Charlie towards Michael. Therefore, social circle impacts personal development and overall well-being.

o Chapter 7:
- This chapter is titled “Be a Life-Long Learner”. Additionally, this chapter continues under the same setting at Mr. Davis’s home, but Charlie imparks another pillar of the concept of becoming a life-long learner. “Formal education will make you a living. Self-education will make you a fortune.” Quoted from Charlie towards Michael, the immaculate observations that many assume graduating from high school or college will make everything they know about life. But the principles from Mr. Davis state that while formal education provides a living, self-education will be more valuable to oneself. Successful people never stop learning, and continuous reading will bring about understanding that develops.

But an ethical issue around this idea is: User Consent and Data Privacy.Ethical concern: AI travel platforms mostly depend on private information such History of locations, Preferences for travel, financial data, and Booking practices. Users may be subject to dangers like identity theft or illegal profiling if their data is gathered or shared without their express consent, violating their right to privacy.
Why it matters: Travel information can provide extremely private details about an individual's routines, travel patterns, and way of life. Misuse erodes confidence and may violate data protection regulations.
Solution is put in place stringent data governance guidelines so that when collecting data, use express opt-in consent. Furthermore, minimize the amount of data you acquire. When feasible, anonymize and encrypt user data. Additionally, clearly and simply describe the usage of data. This preserves user confidence while guaranteeing the ethical treatment of personal data.
